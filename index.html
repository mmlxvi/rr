Welcome to Dr. Reed's Philosophical Counseling website. If you are looking for philosophical, socratic counseling by telephone, we hope that you keep reading. Dr. Reed has been providing philosophical counseling for over fifteen years to many different types of people ranging in age from early teen years to senior citizens. No background in philosophy is required to benefit from philosophical counseling, nor is any particular level of educational attainment. The only requirement is a desire to reflect upon and critically examine one's own life and belief system.

Dr. Reed does not diagnose people as "mentally ill" and does not dispense medication. Rather, he focuses on "problems of living", including but not limited to issues surrounding religious and ethical beliefs, questions of meaning, career choices and dilemmas, addictions, relationships, depression, anxiety, and many other issues as well. He draws from the 3,000+ year history of Western and Eastern philosophies, as well as contemporary fiction and literature, socio-political criticism, social sciences, art, music, and film to address client concerns.

Philosophical counseling is not for everyone, for you as a client must participate in your own process of moving toward your full capacity as a human being - it is not some "cure" effected upon you, but a process in which you co-participate with the philosophical counselor in a journey towards your own progressive liberation.

Dr. Ross Channing Reed holds a B.A. in Philosophy from Millersville University (PA), a M.A. in Philosophy from Baylor University, a M.Mus from The University of Memphis and a Ph.D. in Philosophy from Loyola University of Chicago. His areas of specialization include existentialism, phenomenology, ethics, philosophical psychology, philosophy of religion, and addiction. He has experience working in mental health in both inpatient and outpatient settings.

Dr. Reed also has over 25 years of college and university teaching experience, having taught philosophy, interdisciplinary humanities, global studies, and music at Loyola University Chicago, The School of the Art Institute of Chicago, Columbia College Chicago, Rhodes College, Christian Brothers University, The University of Memphis and Drury University. He began his own private practice in philosophical counseling in 1998 and has had clients from Europe, Asia, Africa and North America. He is fully certified in client counseling by the American Philosophical Practitioners Association. He is a member of the American Society for Philosophy, Counseling, and Psychotherapy as well as the American Philosophical Association.

Dr. Reed is the author of five books: Love and Death: An Existential Theory of Addiction, A Philosopher Reports to Planet Earth, and The Terror of the Simulacra Trilogy (Vituperous Cleavage, Bulbous Chunks, Evisceration).

A Philosopher Reports to Earth is a philosophical tour of many of the major issues of our time. Today, corporations are too big to fail, but you as an individual can fail miserably––and that’s if you do everything right. What hope remains for Americans today? From the health care debacle, to the economic downturn, climate change, addiction, war, terrorism, torture, warrantless surveillance, A Philosopher Reports to Earth addresses them all. Perennial issues are addressed as well: relationships, love, and death.

In Love and Death: an Existential Theory of Addiction, Dr. Reed refers to an eclectic array of thinkers, in particular, existential philosophers Soren Kierkegaard and Jean-Paul Sartre. According to Dr. Reed, addiction is usually the result of existential or life conditions rather than underlying physiological problems. Therefore, it may involve not only drugs or alcohol but also relationships, belief systems, activities, and even emotional states. In this creative and provocative work, Reed argues that Sartre's theory of love is in fact a theory of addiction.

The Terror of the Simulacra trilogy is a story about average people living in 21st century dystopian America, a society in which freedom is just a rumor, a rumor propagated twenty-four hours a day by the sycophantic corporate media––while the average worker is becoming more enslaved to the universal credit-debt system by the hour, making American workers, effectively, indentured servants to the international power elite, the nameless, faceless destroyers of everything that makes life worth living. Could a society in which 99% are effectively marginalized be called a civilization at all?
